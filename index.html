Bachelor Thesis

Augmented Reality

The aim of this study was to investigate whether histogram matching can be used when rendering furniture in augmented reality, when virtual furniture are being placed in a real room. The intention is that the furniture should be perceived as if it were realistically standing in the room.

Histogram matching is a method that modifies the pixel intensities in an image so as to make the image intensity histogram to resemble the histogram of another image.

The histogram matching was best suited for dark environments, and less suited for environments with high contrast. In all the cases we tested, the test participants preferred the histogram matched furniture over the furniture with neutral lighting.

  • Date: Spring 2016
  • Course: Bachelor Thesis
  • Skills: Augmented Reality, Historgram matching
